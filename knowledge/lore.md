================================================================================
  THE SHATTERED SKY — CAMPAIGN COMPENDIUM & WORLD GUIDE
  A Dungeons & Dragons Campaign
================================================================================

One fateful night, a massive rift tore through the heavens — splitting the sky
into jagged, radiant pieces that now drift above a broken world. Where the
shards fall, empires rise and crumble. Where the rifts bleed, ancient horrors
awaken. The sky has shattered. So has everything beneath it.


================================================================================
  WORLD OVERVIEW: A WORLD REWRITTEN
================================================================================

  VII   — Years since the Great Rift
  33    — Named sky shards (catalogued)
  5     — Major skyborne factions
  ∞     — Uncatalogued rifts still bleeding

Seven years ago, the Night of Ten Thousand Cracks shattered the firmament.
Astronomers who survived the event speak of a sound like the world inhaling —
and then silence, as the sky broke apart. The shards that fell did not strike
the earth. Instead, they floated: massive, jagged islands of crystallised
aetheric energy, trailing curtains of azure light. Some are the size of a
market square. The largest, called the Throne, is larger than any city below it.

The civilisations that adapted quickly — those with airship technology, powerful
mages, or skyward-facing faiths — now dominate. Those that did not have
collapsed into ruin, their cities buried under falling debris or swallowed by
the strange new beasts that pour from the rifts.


================================================================================
  THE SHARDS: ANATOMY OF A SKY SHARD
================================================================================

EMBER SHARDS
Burning cores of condensed aetheric fire. Cities beneath them enjoy perpetual
warmth but suffer devastating "fire rains" when the shard trembles. Forges
built on their undersides never cool.

GLACIAL SHARDS
Vast drifting islands of permafrost and preserved pre-rift ruins. Scholars prize
them for the ancient structures sealed perfectly within. The creatures entombed
inside are another matter.

VOID SHARDS
The most feared. They emit no light — only a deep, resonant wrongness. Compasses
spin. Spells misfire. Whatever fell through from the other side of the rift came
through these first.

STORM SHARDS
Perpetually wreathed in crackling lightning. Sky pirates have learned to harvest
their energy. The storm giants that nest on their peaks charge a steep toll in
blood or treasure.

VERDANT SHARDS
Impossible ecosystems of jungle and overgrowth, suspended impossibly in the air.
Home to creatures not seen since before recorded history — and aggressive about
their isolation.

RESONANT SHARDS
Singing with latent magic. Any spell cast within their radius is amplified —
but also unpredictable. The Arcane Consortium would sell entire nations to claim
one outright.


================================================================================
  CAMPAIGN HOOKS: ADVENTURE FRAMEWORKS
================================================================================

HOOK 1 — SHARDS OF POWER
Each shard holds a dangerous secret or imprisoned relic. Powerful factions have
claimed the most accessible ones, and their proxies now contest the rest with
increasingly brutal tactics. The party must navigate a web of political intrigue
— brokering alliances, staging heists against shard-fortresses, and deciding
which powers deserve the weapons they seek. Not every faction is wrong. Not
every faction is survivable.

HOOK 2 — SKYBORNE CREATURES
The rifts have torn open passages from places that do not map onto any known
plane. The creatures that emerge are not inherently evil — they are displaced,
terrified, and powerful. Some have begun to evolve rapidly in this new sky-rich
environment. The party might hunt them, study them, broker peace with the more
intelligent ones — or tame the rarest to build a sky-force no existing faction
can match.

HOOK 3 — ANCIENT PROPHECIES
The shattering was not random. The pre-rift civilisation — the Caelari,
sky-builders whose floating cities were destroyed in the catastrophe — left
warning texts scattered across buried libraries and sealed vaults. The party
must piece together their message before the final sequence of events the
Caelari feared comes to pass. They predicted the shattering. They predicted
something worse would follow.

HOOK 4 — FLOATING CITIES
Some cities rebuilt themselves on shard-platforms, growing rich and strange in
the seven years since. Shard-City Velanthos is a labyrinthine merchant empire
built across three connected shards. Below, the ruins of old capital Caedra are
slowly being consumed by something that lives in the dark underneath the largest
Void Shard. The party's map of the world becomes obsolete every season as the
shards slowly drift.


================================================================================
  THE FIVE FACTIONS: POWERS OF THE BROKEN SKY
================================================================================

THE AUREATE COMPACT — Merchant Coalition (Nominally Neutral)
The most powerful commercial force in the post-rift world. They control the
majority of airship lanes and supply chains between the shards. Their neutrality
is a business model, not a moral position — they will sell to anyone who can
pay, and their definition of "payment" gets creative. The Compact funds most of
the independent sky-cartographers who map new shards, which means they often
know about a discovery before any other faction.

THE ARCANE CONSORTIUM — Scholars & Mages (Cautiously Beneficial)
A coalition of mage-guilds, universities, and independent researchers racing to
understand the shards before someone weaponises them all. They are not above
ruthlessness in their pursuit of knowledge — field agents have been known to let
villages burn if it means recovering an ancient artefact. But their goal is
understanding, not conquest, and they are the best-equipped to actually fix the
sky — if such a thing is possible.

THE CLOUDRUNNERS — Sky Pirates & Free Sailors (Chaotic Wild Cards)
Born from the collapse of three naval empires, the Cloudrunners have no king and
no law. They operate from a rotating fleet of converted warships and stolen
sky-platforms. They are the best at getting somewhere no one else can reach, and
they will take you there — for a cut of whatever you find. Some captains have a
code. Some don't. The party will need them eventually.

THE ASCENDANT CHURCH OF THE SHARDS — Religious Zealots (Dangerous Idealists)
They believe the shattering was divine will — a pruning, a selection. Those who
rise to claim the shards are blessed. Those who remain below are not meant to
ascend. Their theology is internally consistent and horrifying. Their
missionaries are persuasive and well-funded. And there is something in their
sacred texts that suggests one of their founders actually knew the rift was
coming.

THE IRON SOVEREIGNTY — Military Empire (Clear Antagonists)
The only surviving pre-rift empire, they regard the shattering as a catastrophe
that must be corrected through force. Their solution: harvest every shard,
contain every rift, and restore the old order — under their rule, naturally.
They have the largest standing army and the most airships. They also have a
secret: their emperor died in the shattering. Someone, or something, is ruling
in his place.


================================================================================
  KEY LOCATIONS: WHERE THE SKY MEETS THE WORLD
================================================================================

THE THRONE — Shard Prime
The largest shard, trailing ten miles of crystallised blue fire. Three rival
factions have built fortresses on it. The ruins of a Caelari city lie at its
core — most of it still unexplored, because the things that live in the ruins
keep killing the scouts. Whoever fully claims the Throne effectively claims
the sky.

VELANTHOS — City of Three Shards
A mercantile city sprawling across three connected shards, linked by a web of
suspension bridges and aether-cables. It is the most cosmopolitan place in the
post-rift world and the most dangerous. Forty percent of its population arrived
in the last two years. No one agrees on what the law actually is.

THE DROWNING RUINS — Caedra Below
The old capital, half-submerged where a rift opened beneath its foundations.
Accessible only at low tide — which has become irregular since the shattering.
The Arcane Consortium wants what's in the libraries. The Iron Sovereignty wants
what's in the vault below the library. The thing that lives in the water wants
everything.

THE SILENCE — Void Shard XVII
Nothing broadcasts from within a two-mile radius. Birds don't fly near it.
Messages in bottles thrown from its edges arrive empty, the ink having simply
vanished. The Consortium has sent six expeditions. Three returned. None of the
survivors agreed on what they saw, and all three developed an aversion to
looking at the sky afterward.

THE ANCHOR ENGINE — Unknown Location
A device of Caelari construction that supposedly could re-seal the rifts. Every
faction is looking for it. The Ascendant Church wants to destroy it, believing
its use would deny the divine will. The Consortium wants to study it. The Iron
Sovereignty wants to use it — though whether re-sealing the sky would actually
restore the old world is unknown, and some mages quietly fear it would make
things considerably worse.

THE UNDERBELLY FLEET — Cloudrunner Territory
Not a place so much as an ever-shifting constellation of ships moored to shard
undersides where no one else can follow. The Cloudrunners' informal capital. Has
a market, three taverns, a prison, and a shipwright who can rebuild any vessel
in three days flat if you have the materials. Finding it requires knowing someone
who lives there.


================================================================================
  POSSIBLE VILLAINS: THE FORCES AGAINST
================================================================================

THE SKYBORNE TYRANT — Primary Antagonist
Arch-Magister Velior Keth, who controlled two shards before the party ever heard
his name. Now he controls seven. His logic is immaculate: only those who can
wield shard-power deserve to rule, and he has proven his fitness. He may not be
wrong about the power. He is absolutely wrong about what he intends to do
with it.

THE FALLEN STAR — Cosmic Threat
Something imprisoned in the firmament since before the gods finished the world.
The shattering didn't just crack the sky — it cracked its cell. It has no name
in any living language. It is not evil in any way that maps onto morality. It
wants to return to what it was. That process would unmake the sun.

THE SHARD CULT — Ideological Force
The Ascendant Church's militant wing, led by a woman who claims she died in the
shattering and was resurrected by the shards themselves. She may be telling the
truth. Her crusade to prevent the rifts from being sealed is gaining followers,
and she has begun making converts inside the Iron Sovereignty's officer corps.


================================================================================
  THE CAELARI WARNING — ANCIENT PROPHECY
================================================================================

  "When the sky remembers what it forgot,
   the shards will sing and the void will speak.
   Seven who rise will become the lock,
   or seven who fall become the breach."

  — Inscription found in Caelari ruin, Shard IX. Translation disputed.


================================================================================
  ADVENTURE SEEDS: SESSION STARTERS
================================================================================

SEED 1 — THE CARTOGRAPHER'S LAST MAP
A famous Consortium cartographer has gone missing. Her last transmission
mentioned finding a shard that doesn't appear on any official registry — one
that's been moving deliberately, like it's being steered. Her apprentice hires
the party to find her. The map she made is already being hunted by three
factions.

SEED 2 — CARGO AND CONSEQUENCE
The party is hired to move a sealed crate from Velanthos to an address in the
Drowning Ruins. Simple job. Except the crate is breathing, the Cloudrunners are
intercepting them, and the address doesn't exist on any map made after the
shattering.

SEED 3 — THE CREATURE TREATY
A skyborne creature of unusual intelligence — a wind-whale, or something
stranger — has begun approaching airships not to attack them but to trade. It
has Caelari artefacts. It wants safe passage through Iron Sovereignty airspace.
The party may be the only ones who can negotiate, if they can figure out how it
communicates.

SEED 4 — THE SHARD THAT FALLS
Orbital decay. One of the mid-sized shards has begun descending — slowly, but
inexorably. A city of 40,000 sits directly beneath it. The Consortium says it
can be stopped, but needs components from three other shards controlled by
hostile factions. The clock is twenty-two days.

SEED 5 — CORONATION DAY
The Iron Sovereignty has announced a public event on their flagship shard: the
coronation of their "restored" emperor. The Compact suspects it's a trap for
rival faction leaders. The Church suspects it's a divine sign. The Consortium
suspects the emperor is not what he appears. The party is invited, and everyone
wants them to attend with secret instructions.


================================================================================
  GAMEPLAY MECHANICS & PILLARS
================================================================================

SHARD ATTUNEMENT
Prolonged exposure to different shard types has physical effects. Characters who
spend significant time near Void Shards may develop sensory anomalies. Ember
Shard exposure grants fire resistance — and an uncomfortable relationship with
enclosed spaces. Attunement is slow, visible, and not easily reversed.

SKYSHIP NAVIGATION
Airships run on compressed shard-energy. Different energy types have different
handling properties. Void-fuel is the fastest and least stable. Verdant-fuel is
the slowest and completely silent. Crews with the wrong fuel for their mission
make dramatic, expensive mistakes.

FACTION STANDING
Every significant action shifts relationships with all five factions
simultaneously. Helping the Consortium access a restricted shard may damage
Compact trading relations. Dealing with the Cloudrunners may be a diplomatic
incident with the Sovereignty. The web of consequences is the campaign.

THE DRIFT CALENDAR
Shards move. The political geography of the sky changes seasonally. A route that
was safe last season may now pass through contested airspace. The party's map is
always somewhat out of date, and some locations only become accessible when the
drift alignment is right.

RIFT EVENTS
New rifts open unpredictably, releasing environmental hazards, creatures, or
fragments of whatever is on the other side of the sky. These are
campaign-disrupting events: a rift opening over Velanthos might reshape the
city, produce a creature that becomes a recurring threat, or reveal a passage
the party needs to close before something worse comes through.

ANCIENT TECHNOLOGY
Caelari artefacts work by principles no living person fully understands. They
can be reverse-engineered, damaged, or misused with spectacular consequences.
Some require the user to be attuned to a shard to function. Some function only
at night. One or two may be sentient in ways that are not obvious until it is
too late.


================================================================================
  NARRATIVE THEMES: THE HEART OF THE CAMPAIGN
================================================================================

  - Exploration & Discovery
  - Political Intrigue
  - Ancient Mysteries
  - Moral Consequence
  - Survival & Adaptation
  - Legacy & Ruin
  - Nature of Power
  - What Was Lost

At its core, The Shattered Sky is a campaign about what people do when
everything changes at once. The sky breaking was an extinction-level catastrophe
that some civilisations survived and even thrived from. The players inhabit that
aftermath: a world that is simultaneously full of wonder — new places to
discover, new powers to wield, creatures never before documented — and deep,
earned grief for what was lost.

The central question the campaign keeps returning to is not "how do we fix the
sky?" — though that is the mechanics of many sessions — but whether fixing it is
the right thing to do. The post-rift world has its own beauty, its own order,
its own people who have built their identities around it. The Caelari caused the
shattering. Undoing their mistake might be the heroic thing. Or it might be the
selfish thing.
